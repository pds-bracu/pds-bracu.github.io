Research Statement

My research is centered at the intersection of nanophotonics, plasmonics, and metasurfaces. I am driven by the challenge of designing and simulating novel nanoscale structures to manipulate light in new ways. My primary focus is on developing advanced photonic devices, specifically ultra-broadband absorbers and high-sensitivity biosensors.

My academic journey started with a deep dive into bio-nanophotonics, where I developed a label-free plasmonic sensor for detecting biomolecules in solution. This work strengthened my understanding of Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) and classical electrodynamics. My ongoing M.Sc. in Electronics and Photonics directly informs my current work, which involves the design of mid-infrared ultra-broadband absorbers with thin geometries for infrared sensing, imaging, and camouflage. My theoretical foundation in classical electrodynamics and light-matter interactions has been crucial in addressing these complex problems.

I am also motivated by the role of photonics in communication technologies, especially as conventional fiber networks approach bandwidth and speed limitations. To address this, I developed a polarization-insensitive, optically engineered metasurface for the 2 µm wavelength band, suitable for fiber coupling and lensing. This work allowed me to explore phase-change materials (PCM) and their role in achieving dynamic control of optical properties. Along this research journey, I have developed expertise in computational electromagnetics and device modeling tools, such as Lumerical FDTD, DEVICE, and COMSOL Heat Transfer.

Moving forward, I aim to extend my experience into more challenging photonics domains with practical implementation. I am interested in fabricating optically engineered materials, exploring reverse design of nanophotonic devices using machine learning, and Exploring PCMs in integrated photonic devices for photonic computing. Together, I hope to contribute to next-generation photonic systems across sensing, communication, and computation at the speed of light.

If any of this sounds interesting, or if you would like to collaborate, please feel free to send me an email!

M.Sc. Thesis (Ongoing)

An Ultra-broadband Thin Infrared Absorber

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Sajid Muhaimin Choudhury

Plasmonic Absorber Mid-IR Lumerical

This thesis (in progress) focuses on the design and simulation of an ultra-broadband plasmonic infrared absorber utilizing a thin, multi-layered dielectric and metallic metasurface structure. The work aims to achieve high absorption across a broad mid-IR range for applications in thermal emitters or energy harvesting.

B.Sc. Thesis (Published)

Structurally Tunable Gear-shaped Plasmonic Biosensor

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Sajid Muhaimin Choudhury

Plasmonic Biosensor FDTD Method Lumerical

Design and FDTD analysis of a structurally tunable gear-shaped plasmonic biosensor. The work demonstrated a high refractive index (RI) sensitivity of 1044 nm/RIU, making it suitable for chemical and biomedical sensing. This work was published in Optics Express.

Other Research Projects

Research Contribution

Polarization insensitive electrically reconfigurable meta-lens for the 2 μm wavelength

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Sajid Muhaimin Choudhury

Flat-Optics PCM Comsol Lumerical

Design and multiphysics simulation of an optically engineered metasurface operating at the 2 µm waveband for next-generation telecommunication. The device, integrating In₃SbTe₂ (IST) and ITO micro-heaters, exhibits dynamic switching between reflective and transmissive modes with a high ON–OFF ratio of 26 dB and low energy consumption. This work was published in Optical Materials Express.

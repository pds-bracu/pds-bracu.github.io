About Me

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at BRAC University and an active Researcher in Nanophotonics and Plasmonics. My work lies at the intersection of optical device design, flat optics, and nonlinear optics, with a primary focus on developing novel plasmonic-based solutions

My teaching journey at BRAC University began in November 2021 as an adjunct lecturer, and I now coordinate key courses in Circuits and Electronics and Electronic Devices and Circuits. I have also had the pleasure of teaching Digital Electronics, Pulse Techniques, and VLSI Design. My academic foundation was built at the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I completed my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering (2021) with a major in Electronics. It was my undergraduate thesis on plasmonic biosensors that first sparked my interest in nanophotonics and optical device design. This passion has carried forward into my M.Sc., which I am currently pursuing in Electronics and Photonics at BUET. My current thesis work focuses on the design of a broadband plasmonic absorber. In both my teaching and research, I value a methodical, curiosity-driven approach. I aim to turn theoretical concepts into practical and innovative applications. I also believe that collaboration and clear communication are essential to success.

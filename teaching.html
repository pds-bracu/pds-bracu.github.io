Teaching Portfolio

Teaching, to me, is the art of turning understanding into inspiration. My background in Electronics has shaped the way I think, question, and explain. I have always been curious by nature, driven to explore the “why” behind every concept I learn. Equally, I find real joy in breaking down those concepts and helping others see how things truly work. I began teaching at BRAC University in November 2021, where I have primarily taught CSE250: Circuits and Electronics and CSE251: Electronic Circuits and Devices. These courses reflect my academic roots and my passion for circuit theory and electronic systems. Beyond teaching, I also coordinate both the CSE250 and CSE251 courses, managing course structure, materials, and overall learning design. I place great value on creating quality teaching materials, because I believe that effective learning begins with well-crafted resources. From detailed lecture slides to guided lab notebooks, I strive to design materials that make complex topics approachable and engaging. I also believe that education should be as open and accessible as possible. Many of my lectures and resources are freely available to the public, with over 100 hours of recorded content shared on my YouTube channel. Watching students grow in confidence and gradually master challenging ideas is one of the most fulfilling parts of my career. It is a reminder that good teaching is not just about sharing knowledge, it is about nurturing curiosity and understanding in others.

Over 100 hours of my recorded lectures are publicly available on my YouTube Channel.
